Learn About Skincare Acids

Niacinamide

Skin Type: All skin types, especially oily & acne-prone.

Benefits: Reduces inflammation, minimizes pores, regulates oil, and evens skin tone.

Skin Concerns: Acne, dark spots, redness, dullness.

How It Works: Strengthens the skin barrier and improves hydration.

Best Combinations:

  • + Hyaluronic Acid → Best for Dry Skin
  • + Vitamin C → Fades Dark Spots & Evens Skin Tone
  • + Retinol → Treats Acne & Wrinkles
  • + Salicylic Acid → Controls Oil & Fights Acne
  • + Alpha Arbutin → Reduces Pigmentation & Dark Spots

Acid Combinations to Avoid: Avoid combining with Vitamin C (may cause irritation when used together).

Lactic Acid

Skin Type: Dry and sensitive skin.

Benefits: Hydrates, exfoliates, and brightens.

Skin Concerns: Dull skin, uneven texture, dryness.

How It Works: Gently removes dead skin cells while maintaining moisture.

Best Combinations:

  • + Niacinamide → Improves Texture & Brightens Skin
  • + Hyaluronic Acid → Deep Hydration
  • + Vitamin C → Boosts Glow & Reduces Pigmentation

Acid Combinations to Avoid: Avoid combining with other strong exfoliants like Glycolic Acid to prevent irritation.

Salicylic Acid

Skin Type: Oily and acne-prone skin.

Benefits: Unclogs pores, reduces acne, and controls oil.

Skin Concerns: Acne, blackheads, whiteheads.

How It Works: Exfoliates deep into pores to remove excess oil.

Best Combinations:

  • + Niacinamide → Best for Oily & Acne-Prone Skin
  • + Zinc → Regulates Oil & Soothes Skin

Acid Combinations to Avoid: Avoid combining with Glycolic Acid as it may lead to over-exfoliation.

Hyaluronic Acid

Skin Type: All skin types, especially dry skin.

Benefits: Provides hydration, plumps skin, and reduces fine lines.

Skin Concerns: Dryness, dehydration, fine lines.

How It Works: Retains moisture for long-lasting hydration.

Best Combinations:

  • + Niacinamide → Hydrates & Strengthens Skin Barrier
  • + Lactic Acid → Deeply Moisturizes & Exfoliates

Acid Combinations to Avoid: Generally safe with other acids, but avoid combining with strong exfoliants for sensitive skin.

Glycolic Acid

Skin Type: Normal, combination, and oily skin.

Benefits: Exfoliates, brightens, smooths skin texture.

Skin Concerns: Dullness, hyperpigmentation, fine lines.

How It Works: Removes dead skin and boosts collagen.

Best Combinations:

  • + Hyaluronic Acid → Hydration & Exfoliation
  • + Vitamin C → Enhances Glow & Reduces Pigmentation

Acid Combinations to Avoid: Avoid using with other strong acids (like Salicylic Acid) to prevent irritation.
